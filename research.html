Deep Learning Indaba 2026 / IJCAI Special Volume

Data-Driven Lifelines: An Artificial Intelligence and Econometric Early-Warning System for Small and Medium-Sized Enterprise Survival in Cameroon

Konlack Lonlack, G., TIAO, E.

Impact: Deployed as an interactive policy dashboard at the National Institute of Statistics. Identifies 80% of enterprises at high failure risk 12–24 months ahead, enabling targeted policy intervention.

An early-warning system for SME survival integrating econometric inference, ensemble machine learning and survival analysis on national enterprise census data. CatBoost reaches AUC = 0.884; a Random Survival Forest provides firm-level survival curves.

ACCEPTED · 2026

Deep Learning Indaba 2026 / IJCAI Special Volume

FloodTriage: A Decision-Theoretic Tri-Fold Deferral Policy Towards Human-in-the-Loop Streamflow Forecasting

Gohouedé, L.C., Gohouedé, M.A.L., TIAO, E., Edou, J.R.

Impact: Reduces false flood alarms (which cause evacuations) while maintaining 100% miss detection; routes only 25% of predictions to humans for review, enabling rapid response in resource-constrained settings.

A human–AI collaboration framework for flood forecasting on the Ouémé River (Benin): an LSTM deep ensemble governed by a tri-fold routing gateway that decomposes deferral into epistemic, aleatoric and Knightian uncertainty signals.

Trustworthy Human-AI Collaboration for SME Survival: A Cost-Aware and Uncertainty-Guided Deferral Framework

TIAO, E., Gohouedé, M.A.L., Gohouedé, L.C., Edou, J.R., Mwanda, V.

Key innovation: Cost-sensitive routing that decomposes model uncertainty to decide which predictions to automate and which to defer — balancing expected cost, fairness criteria and undetected-failure risk.

A framework optimizing human-AI collaboration in high-stakes predictions by decomposing uncertainty into model confidence, data scarcity signals and unknowns, then routing decisions based on business cost and fairness constraints.

Master's research project (Sep 2025–Jul 2026) — Probabilistic forecasting of electricity consumption and solar PV production with explainable uncertainty attribution. Methods: quantile regression, conformal prediction, SHAP-based attribution. Deployed for use in production energy systems.

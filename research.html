DeepSemantics at SemEval-2026 Task 9: Label-Wise Optimization with Adaptive Focal Loss for Polarization Manifestation Identification

TIAO, E., Edou, J.R., Gohouedé, M.A.L.

A multilingual system for detecting fine-grained polarization manifestations in English and Hausa social media posts. Combines transformer encoders with Adaptive Focal Loss and label-wise threshold optimization to handle severe class imbalance. Ranked 5th on the Hausa track and 14th on the English track.

Data-Driven Lifelines: An Artificial Intelligence and Econometric Early-Warning System for Small and Medium-Sized Enterprise Survival in Cameroon

Konlack Lonlack, G., TIAO, E.

An early-warning system for SME survival integrating econometric inference, ensemble machine learning and survival analysis on national enterprise census data. CatBoost reaches AUC = 0.884; a Random Survival Forest provides firm-level survival curves, deployed as an interactive policy dashboard.

Deep Learning Indaba 2026 / IJCAI Special Volume ACCEPTED · 2026

FloodTriage: A Decision-Theoretic Tri-Fold Deferral Policy Towards Human-in-the-Loop Streamflow Forecasting

Gohouedé, L.C., Gohouedé, M.A.L., TIAO, E., Edou, J.R.

A human–AI collaboration framework for flood forecasting on the Ouémé River (Benin): an LSTM deep ensemble governed by a tri-fold routing gateway that decomposes deferral into epistemic, aleatoric and Knightian uncertainty signals — reducing flood misses while deferring only a quarter of timesteps.

Trustworthy Human-AI Collaboration for SME Survival: A Cost-Aware and Uncertainty-Guided Deferral Framework

TIAO, E., Gohouedé, M.A.L., Gohouedé, L.C., Edou, J.R., Mwanda, V.

A cost-sensitive routing framework that decomposes model uncertainty to decide which SME failure predictions to automate and which to defer to human specialists — balancing expected cost, fairness criteria and undetected-failure risk.

Master's research project — Probabilistic forecasting of electricity consumption and solar PV production with explainable uncertainty attribution (AIMS / Stellenbosch, 2026). Methods: quantile regression, conformal prediction, SHAP-based uncertainty attribution.
